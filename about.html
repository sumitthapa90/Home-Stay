    2. Aghanjar Mahadev.

    Aghanjar Mahadev

    Humble Hindu temple honoring Shiva, set in front of a serene stream running over large boulders. Tucked away at the foothills of Dhauladhar and dedicated to Lord Shiva, Aghanjar Mahadev Temple offers peace and serenity to pilgrims and tourists alike. Surrounded by lush forests and emerald mountain peaks, the allegedly 500-year-old temple attracts nature lovers and photography buffs as well. If you bring a picnic basket, there's a picturesque spot by a stream some 50 steps below the shrine. Don't leave before visiting a small sacred cave near the temple, which houses a shivalinga. Make Aghanjar Mahadev Temple a centerpiece of your Dharamsala vacation

    3. Indru Nag Temple

    Indru Nag Temple

    This ancient temple Indru Nag Temple is actually at Indru Nag, in Dharamshala, Himachal Pradesh. It is dedicated to the Snake King and it is believed that he saves the villagers from Natural disasters, especially from heavy rain if they offer prayers to him. You can witness the panoramic landscapes of Dhauladhar and Dharamshala from the hilltop position of the Temple.

    The magnificent location of Indru Nag Temple and spiritual positive energy attract tourists from various places throughout the year. DHARMASHALA TOUR PACKAGE should include this holy and glorious place without any doubt indeed.

    4. Triund.

    Triund

    Triund is a small hill station in the Kangra district in the state of Himachal Pradesh, India. Triund is a part of Dharamkot. Triund is at the foot of the Dhauladhar ranges and is at a height of 2,828 m.

    Triund Trek is one of the most popular treks to go over a weekend from Delhi and Chandigarh. It is perhaps the easiest Himalayan trek to do on your own. With breathtaking views of the Kangra valley and the snow-covered Dhauladhar ranges, it is also very picturesque. Triund is the ideal destination for those seeking a hassle-free taste of trekking in the Himalayas. The trek can easily be done over a weekend.

    The trail to Triund is short but steep. The steep climb is well-compensated by refreshing walks amongst rhododendron and oak trees. There are also various songbirds in the forests along the trail which make it a birdwatcher‚Äôs delight. Considering it‚Äôs an easy trek and with the effortless accessibility from Dharamshala or Mcleodganj, there are a large number of trekkers who go this trek, making it difficult to enjoy the serenity of the place and can get crowded on top during the weekends or holiday season. It is better to experience the beauty of this trek during the weekdays. The town of McLeodganj (also known as Little Lhasa) also provides for a grand opportunity to experience the vibrant Tibetan culture.
